About Me

I’m Ta'Bless Lewis, a Black woman in STEM from the Dallas–Fort Worth area, navigating computer engineering, web design, and game creation while juggling real‑life hurdles. I’ve had an uneven academic path, but each step has confirmed that I’m most at home in hands‑on tech and creative problem‑solving.

My interest in computers started early – from being told in preschool that I couldn’t “be a mermaid” as a job, to proving that I could still build my own kind of magic through technology. At Ritenour High School in Missouri, I took a cybersecurity class that let me work with Linux, macOS, and Windows virtual machines. We tracked down security issues, fixed permissions, removed malware, and used command‑line tools to secure networks. That class is where I realized I love troubleshooting and digging into why systems break.

Later, even when I missed out on more formal CS classes at other schools, I kept gravitating toward creative tech – HTML and simple animations, making custom content and mods for The Sims, Vocaloid and MikuMikuDance projects, and 3D posing and graphic edits. I’m heavily influenced by K‑pop, Japanese alternative fashion, and pastel goth / yume kawaii aesthetics, and I see games as spaces for self‑expression as much as escape.

My dream work is to combine all of that into fashion‑focused, social, and safe experiences: a modern MMO‑style fashion sandbox, privacy‑aware tools, and everyday services like rideshare that feel fair and predictable instead of chaotic. This portfolio is where I connect those ideas with the engineering lab projects and skills that sit behind them, so you can see both the story and the technical side of who I am.

Core Business Aspirations

I’m focused on building practical, real‑world products for everyday people: a rideshare platform that makes commuting predictable and affordable, a privacy‑first verification device, and a fashion sandbox game that treats style as a form of play. These three passion projects are my crown jewels: the ideas I want to grow into long‑term ventures. The rideshare platform is the core business, and the privacy device and fashion sandbox game grew out of the same need to make everyday life fairer, safer, and more fun.

🚗 Subscription‑Based Rideshare for Everyday Commuters

A rideshare company concept built for people who rely on transport every day but can’t afford unpredictable surge pricing. Instead of random routes, the system works with existing public transport lines – trains, buses and other infrastructure – and layers shared vehicle routes on top.

Riders can subscribe to a fixed monthly or yearly plan based on projected fuel costs, commute time, and time‑of‑day patterns in their area. The goal is to give everyday commuters a predictable, affordable option, while still allowing à la carte rides for trips that fall outside the usual routes.

There are no surprise surges and no stranded airport rides that suddenly jump from a fair price to something unreasonable. Rates are transparent to both drivers and riders, and group rides can become cheaper as more people share the vehicle.

🔐 NFC Contact Verification Device (Privacy Spin‑Off)

While designing the rideshare platform, I started exploring how people could prove key details about themselves without handing over all their data to every service. That led to a concept for an NFC‑style device that checks identity against trusted records rather than storing sensitive information directly.

The idea is that a physical card or chip could link to an existing, secure account with a trusted provider. Verification happens by referencing that account, not by copying data into every new app. Owners can toggle whether the device is allowed to respond to taps, similar to turning contactless payments on or off.

It’s meant to work alongside phones, PCs and future NFC readers as they become more common, helping reduce the risk of data leaks and account takeovers without becoming another place your information is stored or sold.

👗 Fashion Sandbox & Dress‑Up Game

A fashion sandbox and dress‑up game concept that combines avatar customization, capsule wardrobes and social runway events. Instead of the usual grindy MMO loops, the focus is on styling, expression and smart systems for outfits and fashion‑driven events.

This project pulls from modern fashion, K‑pop and gaming culture and gives me a place to experiment with UI, player feedback, and custom content ideas. The deeper mechanics and visuals live off this site, but this overview marks it as one of my main creative business goals.
